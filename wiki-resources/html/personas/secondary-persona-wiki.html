Detailed Description

Kareen Miklos is a Kindergarten educator in Berlin, Germany. Since she was a young little girl, her dream was to became an educator. In order to pursue her dream she followed University studies in Applied Psychology, where she developed skills and aptitudes that are generally required in this field of work.

Kareen is married and has two children, of eight and ten respectively. She tries to spend as much time as possible with them, inspiring them to develop their creativity and enhance their learning process. Since she has studies in applied psychology, she knows that in order for her children to obtain good academic results they must follow the rules of a balanced life. This includes doing recreational activities.

One of the ways in which Kareen likes most to use her spare time with her children is to play games together. Thus, she is always in seek of new games that she can play with them. Moreover, she tries to make them feel comfortable in this era of advanced technology through the use of non-conventional interface in the games they play (i.e. gestural, locomotion, etc.).

From another point of view, Kereen is interested in finding new ways to engage kindergarteners to collaborate with each other and develop communication skills. Forming pairs and playing games is a common methods she uses in order to achieve this goal.
