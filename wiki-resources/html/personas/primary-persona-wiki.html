Over the time Michel has developed an affinity towards computer technology, with a particular interest in games.

Being a competitive person in nature, he uses games as a mean to engage in fun competitions with his friends, using social networks to brag about his achievements.

Detailed Description

Michel is a ten-year-old boy from Lyon, France. After observing his extraordinary intellectual capabilities, his parents decided to send him to The International School of Paris (ISP), a private school based in Paris, France. In this stimulating environment, Michel began to cultivate his mind and became one of the best students.

Attending computer technology courses -- since the school is equipped with a computer technology lab -- Michel developed a specific interest towards computer technology. After talking with his parents about his interests, they decided to buy him a laptop which he can use both to study for school and also experience new technologies and have fun.

Thus, he always wants to find out more about how computers work; likewise he is always in seek for <>new and interesting games, which he can use to have fun in his spare time.

Being a competitive person in nature, one of the methods he uses to exteriorise itself is is to challenge his colleagues to play games against him. This enables him to evidentiate as a dominant person, by winning the games against his colleagues.

Michel also has an appeal to new technologies. He is fascinated by the new technologies that enable him new means of interaction with the computer. He also uses social networks to keep in touch with his friends and sometimes also uses them to brag about his achievements or similar things.

Since he is far away from home and his parents, Michel tries to find new and fun methods to spend with them when at home. Most of the time he wants to just stay in the comfort of his home, playing collaborative games with them.

Scenarios
Michel plays a game with his father for the first time

  1. Michel goes to the web page and opens the game on his laptop;

  2. The application detects and displays all the input devices that are connected, and from which it can receive input;

  3. Michel selects the option to start a new game;

  4. A splash screen appears informing Michel that he and his father can watch a demo regarding how to play the game;

  5. Michel approves and he watches the demo with his father;

  6. After they learn how to play the game, they proceed and start controlling tetris pieces.

Modifying the speed of the game

  1. Michel selects the game settings button from the main menu;

  2. After the button is selected a menu with several options appears;

  3. Next, Michel select the modify game speed option from the menu;

  4. This choice leads the boy to a new menu, where he can select the desired speed using a range input;

  5. While adjusting the range input, Michel can see in another frame a tetris piece moving at the currently selected speed; the piece adapts dynamically its speed according to the selection in the range input.

Sharing a high-score using a social network

  1. Michel ends playing a game, making a new high-score;

  2. After finishing the game, a modal interface appears to him presenting his achievement and different social networks on which he can share his high-score;

  3. He selects a social network, after which the application shares his high-score, informing Michel with a corresponding dialog.
